Neural Networks for Predictive Adaptive Optics

I'm currently working on my Masters project with the Centre for Advanced Instrumentation in Durham Universtiy Physics Department. Under the supervision of Dr Nigel Dipper and Dr Andrew Reeves I'm attemtping to use Neural Networks to reduce latency on Astronomical Adaptive Optics

Neural Networks

Neural networks are based on the idea of simulating a mamalian brain. Layers of neurons are connected together with synapses which allow information to flow through the network. By training the network with a series of datasets, the weights connecting indidual neurons can be tuned to produce desired outputs. Neural Networks have the ability to extract patterns and spot trends in complex datasets which cannot be seen.

Adaptive Optics

The next generation of Extremely Large Telescopes (ELT's) will be severely affected by atmospheric distortions. Light passing through the atmosphere is broken into speckles. This is what causes stars to twinkle. If the current distortion of the atmosphere can be measured, actuators under the telescope mirror can distort it in real time to correct the measured image.
